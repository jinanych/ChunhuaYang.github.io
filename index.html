Chunhua Yang

Chunhua Yang a Professor of the School of Computer Science and Techonoly at the Qilu University of Technology in Jinan, China. Her research interests include code change understanding, aspect-oriented software development, and natural language understanding. She has been working in Qilu University of Technology since he graduated from Shandong University in 1995. She received my MSc and PhD in Shandong University in 2002 and 2010 respectively.

Research

Currently, my research focuses on the understanding of code changes. Since 2016, I've been working on the understanding of the relationships among changes that are committed together. So far I have proposed an approach to improving the efficiency of tree-differencing tools by pruning the AST with hunks, and a differecing tool that extends ChangeDistiller to identify the within-statement code changes.

From 1999 to 2014, I was insterested in the field of aspect-oriented software development, and I proposed an approach to weaving stateful aspects into software architecture, as well as methods to analyze and reason out the influences of aspect weaving.

Before 1999 I was also interested in the field of natural language processing, and I implemented a word-segment approach for written Chinese.
